ABOUT



The Principality of Gilaïe (Dutch: Het Vorstendom van Gilaïe) is a micronation claiming sovereignty over the island of Fugloy. It was founded by His Majesty the Potentate of Gilaïe (G. Verkerke). The Principality of Gilaïe has proclaimed itself a nation, but it is not yet recognized as a country by any recognized national government nor by the United Nations.

Gilaïe is a democratic principality (democratisch vorstendom). The democratic element is added by holding plebiscite referenda regularly on the second Friday of every month. More about the structure of the government.

Please take a moment and explore our webpages and learn more about our country. If you have any questions, or desire further information, please feel free to contact us via the contact form in the lower right corner. In the meantime, welcome and enjoy your visit to our website!

History

Gilaïe was thought up in May 2021 after HM The Potentate watched a video by Jay Foreman and Mark Cooper-Jones titled: ‘How do you start a new country?’ The video intrigued the Supreme Leader, but he did not yet act on the idea (yet). The idea went around a group of friends and one person (whose detail will not be disclosed) pursued the Potentate to actually realise the idea.

Soon after, a group of ministers was formed. These ministers are all prodigies in their own area of expertise. The Minister of Foreign Affairs and Principality Relations, for example, has spent years abroad and knows a thing or two about foreign policy.

While the Council of Ministers was being formed, the Potentate was already actively drafting up constitutional rights and writing the document that would later form the foundation of the Principality of Gilaïe: The Gilaïan Proclamation of Independence.

The Proclamation of Independence was signed by all ministers and the Potentate on June 10, 2021, in Goes (The Netherlands). On this day the Principality of Gilaïe was officially a state.

Legality

No domestic legal system anywhere has the capacity to confer status on a micronation. It is only international law that can recognise a micro nation as a nation-state and thus a legal person in international law. That in turn depends on the practice of nations; if enough nation states recognise the existence of a micro nation as a state, it will become one. And Gilaïe states that just Gilaïe is enough.

There is one very important document that defines in great detail the requirements of being a state: The 1933 Montevideo Convention on the Rights and Duties of States. The Principality of Gilaïe believes that it complies with all the rights and obligations of a State:

(a) a permanent population;
(b) a demarcated area;
(c) public authorities; and
(d) the ability to establish relations with the other States.

On the basis of these basic principles, we ask every other state to grant recognition to the Principality of Gilaïe
